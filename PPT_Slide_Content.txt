SLIDE 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Problem

- Andhra Pradesh manages 45,000+ schools across 26 districts and 670+ mandals
- Infrastructure demand planning is manual, paper-based, and prone to errors
- No systematic way to detect fraudulent or inflated infrastructure requests
- Resource allocation is not data-driven — funds often go where they are not needed most
- No real-time visibility into infrastructure gaps across the state
- Field inspections are disconnected from the demand planning process
- No enrolment forecasting to plan infrastructure proactively

Key Question: How can AI help ensure the right infrastructure reaches the right schools at the right time?

------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3: OUR SOLUTION
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Vidya Soudha — AI-Powered Solution

A mobile-first application that brings AI/ML intelligence to every stage of school infrastructure planning:

1. AI Demand Validation — Automatically detects fraudulent, inflated, or erroneous infrastructure requests using 7 rule-based checks + ML anomaly detection
2. Smart Priority Scoring — Composite AI scoring (0-100) ranks every school by infrastructure need across 4 weighted factors
3. Enrolment Forecasting — Linear regression + cohort progression models predict student enrolment 3 years ahead for proactive planning
4. Role-Based Access — 5 distinct roles from State Director to Headmaster, each with purpose-built dashboards
5. Bilingual Support — Full English and Telugu (తెలుగు) interface

SLIDE 5: AI FEATURE 1 — DEMAND VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI-Powered Demand Validation

The system validates every infrastructure demand through a multi-layered AI pipeline:

7 Rule-Based Checks:
1. Unit Cost Validation — Flags deviations >20% from Samagra Shiksha standard costs
2. Duplicate Detection — Identifies duplicate requests for same infra type and year
3. Enrolment Correlation — Cross-references demand against school size and trend
4. Peer Comparison — Compares against similar schools in the region
5. Zero-Value Check — Rejects invalid physical or financial values
6. Existing Infrastructure Check — Flags requests for already-available facilities
7. Over-Reporting Detection — Identifies schools with excessive multi-type demands

ML Layer: Isolation Forest anomaly detection identifies statistically unusual patterns

Scoring: Each demand receives a score (0-100)
- Approved (>=80) | Flagged (50-79) | Rejected (<50)

Explainable AI: Every decision includes per-rule breakdown showing exactly why a demand was approved, flagged, or rejected

------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6: AI FEATURE 2 — PRIORITY SCORING
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI Priority Scoring

Every school receives a composite priority score (0-100) based on 4 weighted factors:

| Factor                  | Weight | What It Measures                              |
|------------------------|--------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Enrolment Pressure     | 30%    | Student-to-infrastructure ratio (overcrowding)|
| Infrastructure Gap     | 30%    | Count of missing critical facilities          |
| CWSN Needs             | 20%    | Accessibility for Children With Special Needs |
| Accessibility Score    | 20%    | Overall infrastructure accessibility          |

Priority Levels:
- CRITICAL (>80) — Immediate intervention needed
- HIGH (>60) — Urgent attention required
- MEDIUM (>40) — Planned improvement
- LOW (<=40) — Adequate infrastructure

Impact: Enables data-driven fund allocation — directing resources to the most underserved schools first

SLIDE 7: AI FEATURE 3 — ENROLMENT FORECASTING
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Enrolment Forecasting

Predicts student enrolment for the next 3 years to enable proactive infrastructure planning:

Two ML Models:
1. Linear Regression — Fits trend line on historical year-wise enrolment data
2. Cohort Progression — Tracks how student cohorts move through grade levels with grade-specific progression rates

Confidence Intervals:
- Year 1: 90% confidence
- Year 2: 70% confidence
- Year 3: 50% confidence

Offline Fallback: When backend is unavailable, the app computes forecasts locally using historical growth rates — ensuring functionality even without internet

Use Case: A school showing 15% yearly enrolment growth can proactively request additional classrooms BEFORE overcrowding occurs

SLIDE 8: THREE-STAGE VALIDATION PIPELINE
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Three-Stage Validation Pipeline

Stage 1: AI Review (Automated)
- Rule engine + Isolation Forest scores every demand
- Transparent per-rule explanations
- Instant results

Stage 2: Field Assessment (Ground Truth)
- Field Inspector visits the school
- Records actual infrastructure status
- Photos, notes, and condition assessment
- Links inspection data to demand plans

Stage 3: Officer Decision (Human-in-the-Loop)
- District/State Officer reviews AI scores + field data
- Manual Approve / Flag / Reject with notes
- Final decision authority remains with humans

Key Principle: AI assists decision-making without replacing human judgment
